From Yemen’s Highlands to Your Cup

Our story

Our story begins in the misty mountains of Yemen, where coffee was first cultivated over 500 years ago. In 2018, our founder Ahmed Lutfi, a coffee historian and third-generation roaster, trekked through the Haraz Mountains and discovered families still growing coffee using methods unchanged since the 15th century.
We witnessed something tragic - the world had forgotten Yemen's coffee legacy. While the term "mocha" originated from Yemen's Al-Makha port, today less than 1% of global coffee comes from its terraced farms. Most Yemeni farmers were abandoning coffee for qat cultivation due to unfair trade practices. That journey became BlackCoffee. We partner directly with 12 smallholder farms in the Sana'a, Hajjah, and Dhamar regions, paying 3x fair trade prices to revive authentic cultivation. Our farmers still:

  • Hand-pick only ripest cherries.
  • Sun-dry on ancient stone terraces
  • Process using traditional camel-skin methods (for certain premium lots)

Each harvest, we navigate war-torn supply chains to bring these treasures to your cup. Unlike commercial coffees, our beans develop naturally for 2 years in Yemen's high-altitude climate, creating their legendary wine-like complexity.

Today we are:

  • The 1st US importer to offer traceable Yemeni microlots
  • Funding water wells and schools at our partner farms

When you drink our coffee you taste:

  • The birthplace of all coffee culture
  • Uncompromised traditional methods
  • A direct lifeline to Yemen's farming families
